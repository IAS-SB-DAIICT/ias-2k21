Why join IEEE?

IEEE appeared from the union of two engineering societies in the early sixties, the IRE (Institute of Radio Engineers) and the AIEE (American Institute of Electrical Engineers). The IEEE is essentially a society for Electrical and Computer Engineers, but it also welcomes Computer Scientists and Physicists. IEEE is what is known as a technical professional society. Every engineering branch has a technical professional society, Civil Engineering has ASCE (American Society of Civil Engineers), Mechanical Engineering has ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers).

What distinguishes IEEE from this other societies is the fact that IEEE is international. There are IEEE chapters in every continent, and in almost every country, and in those countries where IEEE is not established it has links with the local engineering societies, such as the case with IKE (Institution of Electrical Engineers) in the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. This makes IEEE the largest engineering society in the world.

  1. Spectrum, a monthly magazine of IEEE (single copy costs $ 4, It is FREE with Member Subscription).
  2. All Technical papers are freely accessible to IEEE members
  3. It gives you International Recognition as a member of the most active technical organisation(IEEE).
  4. Helps you do research, get recognition & publish your work to the whole world.
  5. You can have a personal email alias email id(ur_name@ieee.org). Furthermore, it adds value to your resume.
  6. Entry in various international events like IEEE Xtreme, etc.
  7. You can be a part of groups of IEEE SB DA-IICT which are the SIG-embedded Programming, SIG-Mobile Application etc.
  8. It gives you access to Digital library of IEEE, Job site of IEEE and IEEE TV on the internet.
  9. Provides an opportunity to Represent college in Quarterly Meets, other meetings, etc.
  10. Test your quality to lead others.

  • 30th January 2021, Git-GitHub Session

    A session on Git-GitHub was organised by IEEE IAS Student Branch Chapter DAIICT on 30th January, 2021. As college campuses haven’t re-opened yet owing to the Covid-19, this session too, like the last few sessions was held online on the Webex platform.

    Git is a version control system that helps developers to maintain and develop their software in a much-organized manner. GitHub is a web-server and website where one can keep all the code and more than one person can collaborate and code on one project simultaneously.

    Irrespective of specific domains, Git GitHub is a very fundamental thing for students especially of an ICT college like us and therefore this session was organised in order to make everyone, especially the freshers realize its value and help them get started with it. Moreover, this session also served as a stepping stone for the ones planning to get started with open source contributions and programs like GSoC, Outreachy, etc. because the contribution to open source is not possible without basic knowledge of Git Github.

    This session was taken over by Mr. Dushyant Pathak who is a very good orator and is currently pursuing his final year of B.Tech in DAIICT itself. He is a skilled Full Stack Developer and is experienced in many other tools and technologies like MEAN, MERN, Electron, Android and Deep Learning. He also maintains a blog (dkprobes.tech) on Data Structures and Algorithms where he explains various typical algorithms in his own lucid style. He has also interned as a tech analyst at Deutsche Bank and thus has industry-level experience too of these things.

    It started with a brief introduction of Git-GitHub, some introductory commands and then since the session was hands-on, it was taken care that participants were able to follow what was going-on on the screen. The participants raised their queries in the chatbox whenever they got stuck while trying on their own and those queries were readily answered during the session itself. Thereafter, the session moved towards a bit advanced concepts like creating branches, merging them, stashing commits, commands for resetting and rebasing, etc. Concepts like forking an open-source repository and contributing to it by making a Pull request were also covered towards the end of the session.

    Indeed, it was an engaging session where potential developers of DAIICT showed full enthusiasm and made the session a success! As a whole, this session also sparked an interest towards open source among the first yearites and motivated them to get started contributing to it.
